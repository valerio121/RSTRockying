                    &lt;h3&gt;
                        10# Tom Yum Spice Soup&lt;/h3&gt;
                    &lt;p&gt;
                        Ingredients:
                    &lt;/p&gt;
                    &lt;ul&gt;
                        &lt;li&gt;1 pound medium-size shrimp (prawns)&lt;/li&gt;
                        &lt;li&gt;10 mushrooms&lt;/li&gt;
                        &lt;li&gt;1 stalk of lemon grass, lightly pounded and cut into 2 inches long&lt;/li&gt;
                        &lt;li&gt;3 lime leaves&lt;/li&gt;
                        &lt;li&gt;1 teaspoon of salt&lt;/li&gt;
                        &lt;li&gt;2 tablespoons of nam pla (Fish Sauce, or "Nam Pla" in Thai)&lt;/li&gt;
                        &lt;li&gt;3 tablespoons of lime juice&lt;/li&gt;
                        &lt;li&gt;6 prik ki nu ( Thai chili) ,pounded lightly&lt;/li&gt;
                        &lt;li&gt;4 cups of water&lt;/li&gt;
                        &lt;li&gt;½ cup of roughly cut cilantro leaves (Coriander)&lt;/li&gt;
                    &lt;/ul&gt;
                    &lt;p&gt;
                        Process:&lt;/p&gt;
                    &lt;ol&gt;
                        &lt;li&gt;Remove the shrimp shell but leave the tails (for look).&lt;/li&gt;
                        &lt;li&gt;Then cut open the back of each shrimp to remove the veins.&lt;/li&gt;
                        &lt;li&gt;Clean the mushrooms with water and dry them well before wedging each into quarters.&lt;/li&gt;
                        &lt;li&gt;Bring water to boil, then add lemon grass, lime leaves, and shrimps.&lt;/li&gt;
                        &lt;li&gt;When the shrimps turn pink, add mushrooms and salts.&lt;/li&gt;
                        &lt;li&gt;Remove the pot from heat after boil. Add fish sauce, lime juice, and hot peppers
                            to taste.&lt;/li&gt;
                    &lt;/ol&gt;

                    &lt;h3&gt;
                        9# America's Shrimp Creole&lt;/h3&gt;
                    &lt;p&gt;
                        Ingredients:&lt;/p&gt;
                    &lt;ul&gt;
                        &lt;li&gt;1 teaspoon paprika&lt;/li&gt;
                        &lt;li&gt;1 teaspoon pepper&lt;/li&gt;
                        &lt;li&gt;1/2 teaspoon salt&lt;/li&gt;
                        &lt;li&gt;1/2 teaspoon garlic powder&lt;/li&gt;
                        &lt;li&gt;1/4 teaspoon oregano&lt;/li&gt;
                        &lt;li&gt;1/4 teaspoon onion powder&lt;/li&gt;
                        &lt;li&gt;1 pinch cayenne pepper&lt;/li&gt;
                        &lt;li&gt;1 pinch thyme&lt;/li&gt;
                        &lt;li&gt;28 uncooked large shrimp&lt;/li&gt;
                        &lt;li&gt;7 tablespoons chilled butter, cut into 1/2 inch pieces&lt;/li&gt;
                        &lt;li&gt;3 tablespoons Worcestershire sauce&lt;/li&gt;
                        &lt;li&gt;3 tablespoons fresh lemon juice&lt;/li&gt;
                        &lt;li&gt;2 teaspoons chopped garlic&lt;/li&gt;
                    &lt;/ul&gt;
                    &lt;p&gt;
                        Process:&lt;/p&gt;
                    &lt;ol&gt;
                        &lt;li&gt;Combine first 8 ingredients in a large bowl.&lt;/li&gt;
                        &lt;li&gt;Add shrimp and toss to coat.&lt;/li&gt;
                        &lt;li&gt;Melt 3 T.of the butter in a large skillet over medium heat.&lt;/li&gt;
                        &lt;li&gt;Add Worcestershire, lemon juice and garlic and saute about 2 minutes.&lt;/li&gt;
                        &lt;li&gt;Add shrimp and saute until opaque.&lt;/li&gt;
                        &lt;li&gt;Add remaining butter slowly until melted.&lt;/li&gt;
                        &lt;li&gt;Transfer shrimp to serving plate and pour sauce over&lt;/li&gt;
                    &lt;/ol&gt;

                    &lt;h3&gt;
                        8# Peru's PAPA A LA HUANCAINA&lt;/h3&gt;
                    &lt;p&gt;
                        Ingredients:&lt;/p&gt;
                    &lt;ul&gt;
                        &lt;li&gt;8 yellow or while potatoes&lt;/li&gt;
                        &lt;li&gt;Huancaína sauce (spicy Cheese Sauce)&lt;/li&gt;
                        &lt;li&gt;Lettuce leaves&lt;/li&gt;
                        &lt;li&gt;2 hard-boiled eggs&lt;/li&gt;
                        &lt;li&gt;8 large black olives, halved&lt;/li&gt;
                    &lt;/ul&gt;
                    &lt;p&gt;
                        Process:&lt;/p&gt;
                    &lt;ol&gt;
                        &lt;li&gt;Heat a large pot of salted water to boiling and add the potatoes.&lt;/li&gt;
                        &lt;li&gt;Boil potatoes until tender when pierced with a fork.&lt;/li&gt;
                        &lt;li&gt;Drain water from potatoes and let cool.&lt;/li&gt;
                        &lt;li&gt;Slice potatoes and arrange on top of the lettuce leaves.&lt;/li&gt;
                        &lt;li&gt;Pour huancaína sauce over potatoes, and garnish with slices of hard-boiled egg and
                            black olive halves.&lt;/li&gt;
                    &lt;/ol&gt;

                    &lt;h3&gt;
                        5# Suicide chicken wings, America&lt;/h3&gt;
                    &lt;p&gt;
                        Ingredients&lt;/p&gt;
                    &lt;ul&gt;
                        &lt;li&gt;2 1/2 cups Frank's red hot sauce&lt;/li&gt;
                        &lt;li&gt;2 tablespoons garlic powder&lt;/li&gt;
                        &lt;li&gt;2 chicken bouillon cubes (or powder)&lt;/li&gt;
                        &lt;li&gt;1 teaspoon pepper&lt;/li&gt;
                        &lt;li&gt;1/2 teaspoon white pepper&lt;/li&gt;
                        &lt;li&gt;2 tablespoons Tabasco sauce&lt;/li&gt;
                        &lt;li&gt;5 ounces barbecue sauce (any brand you like)&lt;/li&gt;
                        &lt;li&gt;5 ounces honey garlic barbecue sauce&lt;/li&gt;
                        &lt;li&gt;2 tablespoons cayenne pepper&lt;/li&gt;
                        &lt;li&gt;1 teaspoon chili powder&lt;/li&gt;
                        &lt;li&gt;1/4 cup butter&lt;/li&gt;
                        &lt;li&gt;4 tablespoons brown sugar&lt;/li&gt;
                        &lt;li&gt;5 lbs chicken wings (tips removed &amp; split in half.)&lt;/li&gt;
                    &lt;/ul&gt;
                    &lt;p&gt;
                        Process:&lt;/p&gt;
                    &lt;ol&gt;
                        &lt;li&gt;Mix all sauce ingrediens in saucepan and heat to melt butter.&lt;/li&gt;
                        &lt;li&gt;Deep fry wings in small batches and mix with sauce.&lt;/li&gt;
                    &lt;/ol&gt;

                    &lt;h3&gt;
                        4# Chicken Jerk, Caribbean
                    &lt;/h3&gt;
                    &lt;p&gt;
                        Ingredients&lt;/p&gt;
                    &lt;ul&gt;
                        &lt;li&gt;1 1/2 lbs. of chicken pieces&lt;/li&gt;
                        &lt;li&gt;5 chopped green onions&lt;/li&gt;
                        &lt;li&gt;1 chopped onion&lt;/li&gt;
                        &lt;li&gt;2tbsp. of brown sugar&lt;/li&gt;
                        &lt;li&gt;1/2 tsp. of ground cloves&lt;/li&gt;
                        &lt;li&gt;1/2 tsp. of ground allspice&lt;/li&gt;
                        &lt;li&gt;1/2 tsp. of ground nutmeg&lt;/li&gt;
                        &lt;li&gt;1 tbsp. of chopped chandon beni/cilantro&lt;/li&gt;
                        &lt;li&gt;1 tbsp. of chopped thyme&lt;/li&gt;
                        &lt;li&gt;1/4 cup of cooking oil&lt;/li&gt;
                        &lt;li&gt;1/2 cup of distilled white vinegar&lt;/li&gt;
                        &lt;li&gt;1/2 cup of soy sauce&lt;/li&gt;
                        &lt;li&gt;1 minced jalapeno pepper &lt;/li&gt;
                    &lt;/ul&gt;
                    &lt;p&gt;
                        Process&lt;/p&gt;
                    &lt;ol&gt;
                        &lt;li&gt;Blend a combination of all onions, jalapeno, soy sauce, vinegar, oil, allspice,
                            thyme, nutmeg, cloves, chadon beni/cilantro and brown sugar for 15 – 30 seconds.&lt;/li&gt;
                        &lt;li&gt;Coat chicken pieces thoroughly in mixture and seal tightly in bowl overnight to
                            soak.&lt;/li&gt;
                        &lt;li&gt;Preheat grill to high heat or oven to 350 degrees F.&lt;/li&gt;
                        &lt;li&gt;Lightly oil grill and cook chicken 5-10 minutes each until liquid is absorbed/Place
                            in grease glass dish and place in oven for 15-20 minutes and turn chicken. &lt;/li&gt;
                    &lt;/ol&gt;
                    &lt;p&gt;
                        Monitor chicken when baking in oven to ensure chicken does not get too dry. Adjust
                        baking time is necessary.&lt;/p&gt;

                    &lt;h3&gt;
                        3# WOT, Ethiopia
                    &lt;/h3&gt;
                    &lt;p&gt;
                        Ingredients&lt;/p&gt;
                    &lt;ul&gt;
                        &lt;li&gt;1 whole chicken, skinned and cut into serving peices ( or 6 legs and 6 thighs)&lt;/li&gt;
                        &lt;li&gt;8 pounds of onions, chopped FINE&lt;/li&gt;
                        &lt;li&gt;1 pound of mashed garlic&lt;/li&gt;
                        &lt;li&gt;1/4 pound mashed ginger&lt;/li&gt;
                        &lt;li&gt;2 ladles full of Ethiopian chili powder (bere bere)&lt;/li&gt;
                        &lt;li&gt;1 to 1 and a half lbs butter (M. says Ethiopians like to see it swimming on top)&lt;/li&gt;
                        &lt;li&gt;Salt and pepper to taste&lt;/li&gt;
                        &lt;li&gt;Nutmeg or cumin optional.&lt;/li&gt;
                        &lt;li&gt;1 hard-boiled egg per person.&lt;/li&gt;
                    &lt;/ul&gt;
                    &lt;p&gt;
                        Process&lt;/p&gt;
                    &lt;ol&gt;
                        &lt;li&gt;Cook onion over low heat, not in any fat, for a long time, stirring constantly over
                            very low heat until all the water is evaporated. Continue stirring until onions
                            are slightly brown. THEN add the butter; cook about 15-20 minutes still over low
                            heat. Add 1 tbsp hot water from time to time (texture should be kind of pasty).&lt;/li&gt;
                        &lt;li&gt;Add bere bere. If it looks too dry, add hot water and stir. Cook it about 30-45
                            minutes - keep stirring frequently. Keep adding hot water if necessary. Be careful
                            not to let it brown, because chili powder will become bitter. Add garlic and ginger
                            and simmer 15 moreminutes. Add water as needed.&lt;/li&gt;
                        &lt;li&gt;Meanwhile, with your other set of free hands, wash chicken peices well, and let
                            stand in salted in lemoned water. Then squeeze each peice really hard to get out
                            as much water as possible.&lt;/li&gt;
                        &lt;li&gt;Then, make little slits in the meat so the sauce will penetrate. Cook the meat in
                            the sauce until meat tis tender, about 30 minutes. Stir from time to time. All this
                            has been uncovered, by the way. Add spices to taste just before removing from stove.
                            Add 1 hard-boiled egg per person, which have been slit (the eggs, not the people),
                            and cook just long enough to heat through. Serve with lots of bread (injera).
                        &lt;/li&gt;
                    &lt;/ol&gt;

                    &lt;h3&gt;
                        2# Vindaloo, India&lt;/h3&gt;
                    &lt;p&gt;
                        Ingredients&lt;/p&gt;
                    &lt;ul&gt;
                        &lt;li&gt;2 (3 1/2-ounce) bags boil-in-bag brown rice&lt;/li&gt;
                        &lt;li&gt;2 teaspoons peanut oil (or canola oil)&lt;/li&gt;
                        &lt;li&gt;1 large sweet onion (Maui or Vidalia make good choices) or white onion is fine too&lt;/li&gt;
                        &lt;li&gt;2 chicken breasts, but into 1 inch strips&lt;/li&gt;
                        &lt;li&gt;1 1/4 teaspoons garam masala&lt;/li&gt;
                        &lt;li&gt;1/4 teaspoon salt&lt;/li&gt;
                        &lt;li&gt;1 teaspoon mustard seeds&lt;/li&gt;
                        &lt;li&gt;1 inch of fresh ginger&lt;/li&gt;
                        &lt;li&gt;1/2 teaspoon ground red pepper&lt;/li&gt;
                        &lt;li&gt;5 thai peppers (be sure to get as close to the stem as possible, that’s where the
                            spice is!)&lt;/li&gt;
                        &lt;li&gt;1 tablespoon garlic&lt;/li&gt;
                        &lt;li&gt;1/4 cup chicken broth&lt;/li&gt;
                        &lt;li&gt;1 (14.5-ounce) can petite diced tomatoes, undrained&lt;/li&gt;
                        &lt;li&gt;1 (8-ounce) can tomato sauce&lt;/li&gt;
                        &lt;li&gt;2 tablespoons chopped fresh cilantro&lt;/li&gt;
                        &lt;li&gt;1 tablespoon red wine vinegar &lt;/li&gt;
                    &lt;/ul&gt;
                    &lt;p&gt;
                        Process&lt;/p&gt;
                    &lt;ol&gt;
                        &lt;li&gt;Prepare rice according to package directions, omitting salt and fat; set aside.&lt;/li&gt;
                        &lt;li&gt;Heat oil in a large nonstick skillet over medium-high heat. Add onion and to pan;
                            sauté 2 minutes. Combine chicken and next 5 ingredients (through thai pepper); toss
                            well; sauté for 5 minutes or until lightly browned. Add garlic.&lt;/li&gt;
                        &lt;li&gt;Add broth to pan, scraping bottom of pan to loosen browned bits, deglaze with red
                            table wine. Add tomatoes and tomato sauce; cook 3 minutes, stirring occasionally.
                            Remove from heat; stir in cilantro and vinegar. Serve over rice.&lt;/li&gt;
                    &lt;/ol&gt;

                    &lt;h3&gt;
                        1# Sambal Ulek, Indonesia&lt;/h3&gt;
                    &lt;p&gt;
                        Ingredients&lt;/p&gt;
                    &lt;ul&gt;
                        &lt;li&gt;250g (8 oz) fresh red chilies&lt;/li&gt;
                        &lt;li&gt;1 tsp salt&lt;/li&gt;
                        &lt;li&gt;1 tsp soft brown sugar&lt;/li&gt;
                    &lt;/ul&gt;
                    &lt;p&gt;
                        Process&lt;/p&gt;
                    &lt;ol&gt;
                        &lt;li&gt;Heat a heavy frying pan and put in the chilies after 2-3 minutes.&lt;/li&gt;
                        &lt;li&gt;Dry roast over a medium heat for a few minutes.&lt;/li&gt;
                        &lt;li&gt;Cool, then chop finely and pound to a paste with the salt and sugar.&lt;/li&gt;
                        &lt;li&gt;The chili seeds may be taken out, or left in if you want a really fiery relish.
                        &lt;/li&gt;
                        &lt;li&gt;The sambal will keep for a week or so in a jar in the refrigerator.&lt;/li&gt;
                    &lt;/ol&gt;
