                    &lt;p&gt;
                        Epitome of culture, the saree stands for elegance in Indian Women.&lt;/p&gt;
                    &lt;p&gt;
                        Our own creation, the saree is said to be developed both in North and South India
                        as a garment of its own. From Draupadi to the woman of today, the saree has protected
                        and glamorized the female wardrobe.
                    &lt;/p&gt;
                    &lt;p&gt;
                        Traced back to the Indus Valley Civilization (2800–1800 BC), the earliest depiction
                        of the saree is said to be the one worn by a female Indus valley priest wearing
                        a drape.
                    &lt;/p&gt;
                    &lt;p&gt;
                        The Saree, four to nine yards lengthy, is said to be a perfectly designed garment
                        to exhibit the female curves in the right places.&lt;/p&gt;
                    &lt;p&gt;
                        The types of sarees adorned all around the country are as varied as its cultures,
                        religions and languages. Rockying (&lt;a href="http://www.facebook.com/rockyingmag"
                            target="_blank"&gt;Facebook&lt;/a&gt;,&lt;a href="http://www.twitter.com/rockyingmag" target="_blank"&gt;Twitter&lt;/a&gt;)
                        manages to get lifted by the boundless Indian 'aanchal' to travel through the country,
                        its colors, arts and textiles. Slide on ladies (and gentlemen)...&lt;/p&gt;

                        THE CENTRAL WAY&lt;/h3&gt;
                    &lt;h4&gt;
                        Chanderi Sarees&lt;/h4&gt;
                    &lt;p&gt;
                    &lt;/p&gt;
                    &lt;h5&gt;
                        Origin&lt;/h5&gt;
                    &lt;p&gt;
                        Traditionally woven in the city of Chanderi in Madhya Pradesh, this kind is famous
                        for its sarees made with mix of silk and cotton.&lt;/p&gt;
                    &lt;h5&gt;
                        Price&lt;/h5&gt;
                    &lt;p&gt;
                        The more costly ones spot real silver and gold (zari) woven borders costing &lt;b&gt;Rs. 20,000&lt;/b&gt;
                        or more.&lt;/p&gt;
                    &lt;h5&gt;
                        Distinct&lt;/h5&gt;
                    &lt;p&gt;
                        Very light weight and simple, this saree is a must have for every woman. Kareena
                        Kapoor flaunts her simple self in this black Chanderi cotton silk saree. This sure
                        is a sign of royalty.&lt;/p&gt;

                &lt;div class="span5"&gt;
                    &lt;h4&gt;
                        Maheshwari Sarees&lt;/h4&gt;
                    &lt;h5&gt;
                        Origin&lt;/h5&gt;
                    &lt;p&gt;
                        Conceived and designed by Queen Ahilya Bai Holkar herself, Maheshwari were specially
                        crafted 9-yard sarees to be gifted to royal guests and relatives.&lt;/p&gt;
                    &lt;h5&gt;
                        Price&lt;/h5&gt;
                    &lt;p&gt;
                        These can be bought for prices between Rs 1500 and Rs 5000.&lt;/p&gt;
                    &lt;h5&gt;
                        Design&lt;/h5&gt;
                    &lt;p&gt;
                        The designs used on these sarees were said to be inspired by drawings on the Maheshwari
                        fort walls. The ‘Chatai’ (Mat) pattern, ‘Chameli ka phool’ (chameli flower) pattern,
                        the ‘Eent’ (Brick) pattern and the ‘Heera’ (Diamond) pattern are a few popular designs
                        used in these sarees. &lt;b&gt;Distinctive in its five-stripes pallu style&lt;/b&gt;, the Maheshwari
                        Saree re-defines neatness.&lt;/p&gt;

                &lt;div class="span5"&gt;
                    &lt;h4&gt;
                        Kosa/Tussar Silk&lt;/h4&gt;
                    &lt;h5&gt;
                        Origin&lt;/h5&gt;
                    &lt;p&gt;
                        Known for its comfort all around the world, Kosa silk is mainly produced in Jharkhand,
                        Bihar, Chhattisgarh and Madhya Pradesh.&lt;/p&gt;
                    &lt;h5&gt;
                        Fabric&lt;/h5&gt;
                    &lt;p&gt;
                        Made from a thread produced by a worm similar to the silk worm, kosa is obtained
                        naturally in shades of cream, honey, beige, yellow and ash, and is extracted from
                        cocoons specially grown on Arjun and Sal trees.&lt;/p&gt;
                    &lt;h5&gt;
                        Price&lt;/h5&gt;
                    &lt;p&gt;
                        This extremely comfy fabric comes at a cost that depends upon the purity of silk,
                        handwork and accessories used on it. Can be bought for prices starting from Rs 1500
                        to Rs 20, 000 and above.&lt;/p&gt;

                        Sambalpuri&lt;/h4&gt;
                    &lt;h5&gt;
                        Origin&lt;/h5&gt;
                    &lt;p&gt;
                        This traditional hand-woven Ikkat (Tie and Dye) Sari is mainly produced in Orissa.&lt;/p&gt;
                    &lt;h5&gt;
                        Distinct&lt;/h5&gt;
                    &lt;p&gt;
                        Known for its incorporation of conventional motifs like shankha (shell), chakra
                        (wheel) and phool (flower),&lt;b&gt;the high point of this saree remains its craftsmanship
                            of ‘Bandhakala’&lt;/b&gt;, the Tie-Dye art reverberated in its complex weaving. To
                        our surprise, the &lt;b&gt;tie and dye is performed on threads and then later woven into a
                            fabric. Phew!&lt;/b&gt;

                    &lt;h4&gt;
                        Jamdani Sarees&lt;/h4&gt;
                    &lt;h5&gt;
                        Origin&lt;/h5&gt;
                    &lt;p&gt;
                        Historically referred to as muslin, the Jamdani weaving custom originates in Bengal.&lt;/p&gt;
                    &lt;h5&gt;
                        Price&lt;/h5&gt;
                    &lt;p&gt;
                        This handloom woven fabric made of fine, pure cotton is a bit of an expensive affair.
                        In 1903, when a silk saree cost as little as Rs. 5, a Jamdani costed 500 rupees.&lt;/p&gt;
                    &lt;h5&gt;
                        Distinct&lt;/h5&gt;
                    &lt;p&gt;
                        Trendy and classy, Jamdani is majorly sought after by fashionable professional women.
                        Elegance woven, is what we call it!&lt;/p&gt;

                        Kantha Sarees&lt;/h4&gt;
                    &lt;p&gt;
                    &lt;/p&gt;
                    &lt;h5&gt;
                        Origin&lt;/h5&gt;
                    &lt;p&gt;
                        Traditional art of a few thousand years ago, Kantha literally means ‘quilt’ in Bengali.
                        This ‘Quilt Art’ (popularly known as Nakshi Kantha) of Bengal, is patterned running
                        stitching that covers the entire surface of a piece.&lt;/p&gt;
                    &lt;h5&gt;
                        Distinct&lt;/h5&gt;
                    &lt;p&gt;
                        Stunningly beautiful portrayal of real life on fabric, no Kantha piece is ever repeated.
                        Bong girl Raima walks the ramp in this sizzling red Kantha saree. Homage in style!&lt;/p&gt;

                        Baluchari Sarees&lt;/h4&gt;

                        Origin&lt;/h5&gt;
                    &lt;p&gt;
                        Popularly known for its embroidery style of human figures, this saree originates

                        Distinct&lt;/h5&gt;
                    &lt;p&gt;
                        The body is entirely covered with small butis and a beautiful floral design runs
                        across the edges. The pallu carries detailed designs depicting narrative motifs,
                        such as those of Radha-Krishna.&lt;/p&gt;

                &lt;div class="span5"&gt;
                    &lt;h4&gt;
                        Tangail Saree&lt;/h4&gt;
                    &lt;h5&gt;
                        Design/Fabric&lt;/h5&gt;
                    &lt;p&gt;
                        Based on the Jamdani weaving styles, Tangail is an opaque and heavier saree made
                        of both cotton and silk fabrics. Cautiously weaved patterns in this saree give out
                        an embroidered effect. Uniformly textured, Tangail has extra warp in the border.&lt;/p&gt;
                    &lt;h5&gt;
                        Origin&lt;/h5&gt;
                    &lt;p&gt;
                        Coming from the villages of West Bengal, Tangail is very popular in east India.&lt;/p&gt;
                    &lt;h5&gt;
                        Price&lt;/h5&gt;
                    &lt;p&gt;
                        The total turnover of Tangail saris exceeds Rs.15crores per annum in a price range
                        of Rs. 100 to Rs. 1500 and above.&lt;/p&gt;

                    &lt;h4&gt;
                        Mekhela Chador&lt;/h4&gt;
                    &lt;h5&gt;
                        Origin&lt;/h5&gt;
                    &lt;p&gt;
                        Popularly known as ‘Mekhela Chador’ , Mekhla is the traditional attire of Assamese
                        women.&lt;/p&gt;
                    &lt;h5&gt;
                        Distinct&lt;/h5&gt;
                    &lt;p&gt;
                        Assam receives a geographical indication due to its production of Muga silk. The
                        Muga silk fabric cannot be dyed or bleached and is worn in its natural golden color.
                        The fabric can be hand-washed and is said to gain lustre after every wash. It is
                        also believed that the fabric is so durable that it often lasts longer than its
                        owner.&lt;/p&gt;
                    &lt;p&gt;
                        This is a silk saree that doesn’t need dry cleaning. Yay!&lt;/p&gt;
                    &lt;h5&gt;
                        Fabric
                    &lt;/h5&gt;
                    &lt;p&gt;
                        These are made in cotton and silk fabrics. Assam silk refers to three kinds of silks:
                        Muga, Pat and Eri. Muga being the most famous for its glossy fine texture and durability.&lt;/p&gt;
                    &lt;h5&gt;
                        Price&lt;/h5&gt;
                    &lt;p&gt;
                        The cotton ones can be bought for Rs. 500 and above the silk ones would cost Rs.
                        1000 and above.&lt;/p&gt;

                        Bandhni Sarees&lt;/h4&gt;
                    &lt;h5&gt;
                        Origin&lt;/h5&gt;
                    &lt;p&gt;
                        Bandhni is the most popular form of tie and dye practiced mainly in Rajasthan and
                        Gujarat.&lt;/p&gt;
                    &lt;h5&gt;
                        Distinct&lt;/h5&gt;
                    &lt;p&gt;
                        Use of only dark colors and mostly red and black backgrounds, is peculiar to this
                        style of tie and dye. Millions of tiny knots called ‘Bheendi’ are tied to give out
                        a beauty as this.&lt;/p&gt;
                    &lt;h5&gt;
                        Price&lt;/h5&gt;
                    &lt;p&gt;

                    &lt;h5&gt;
                        Origin&lt;/h5&gt;
                    &lt;p&gt;

                        of Uttar Pradesh.&lt;/p&gt;
                    &lt;h5&gt;
                        Design/Fabric&lt;/h5&gt;
                    &lt;p&gt;
                        Doria meaning thread, Kota sarees made of pure cotton and silk, have thousands of
                        square patterns called ‘Khats’ on them.&lt;/p&gt;

                        Distinct&lt;/h5&gt;

                    &lt;h5&gt;
                        Design&lt;/h5&gt;
                    &lt;p&gt;
                        Characterized by borders of an oblique square design and a pallu with a peacock

                        Distinct&lt;/h5&gt;

                        UP NORTH&lt;/h3&gt;
                    &lt;h4&gt;
                        Banarsi Sarees&lt;/h4&gt;
                    &lt;h5&gt;
                        Distinct&lt;/h5&gt;
                    &lt;p&gt;
                        One of the finest saris in India, Banarsi is known for its &lt;b&gt;gold and silver brocade&lt;/b&gt;
                        or zari, fine silk and grand embroidery.&lt;/p&gt;
                    &lt;h5&gt;
                        Origin/Fabric&lt;/h5&gt;
                    &lt;p&gt;
                        Originating in the Varanasi district of Uttar Pradesh, there are four main varieties
                        of Banarsi sarees based on the fabric used-Pure Silk (Katan), Organza (Kora), Georgette
                        and Shattir.&lt;/p&gt;
                    &lt;h5&gt;
                        Design/Price&lt;/h5&gt;
                    &lt;p&gt;
                        Jangla, tanchui, Vaskat, Cutwork, Tissue and butidar are the many designs of Banarsi
                        sarees ranging from Rs. 1000 to Rs. 3,40,000 and above.&lt;/p&gt;

                        Distinct&lt;/h5&gt;
                    &lt;p&gt;
                        Too heavy to be worn casually, this saree marks the heaviest brocade work executed
                        on dark shade fabrics like red and maroon best suited for weddings.&lt;/p&gt;

                &lt;div class="span5"&gt;
                    &lt;h4&gt;
                        Chikankari&lt;/h4&gt;
                    &lt;h5&gt;
                        Origin&lt;/h5&gt;
                    &lt;p&gt;
                        Believed to have been premised by Nur Jahan, Mughal emperor Jahangir’s wife, Chikan
                        literally means embroidery. Derived from the Persian word ‘Chakin’ meaning a cloth
                        wrought with needle work, Chikan was basically invented in Lucknow.&lt;/p&gt;
                    &lt;h5&gt;
                        Fabric/Design&lt;/h5&gt;
                    &lt;p&gt;
                        Performed mainly on cotton, georgette and silk fabrics, Chikankari is carried out
                        with block printing patterns on the ground fabric, then stitched by the embroiderer
                        and later washed to remove traces of the print.&lt;/p&gt;

                    &lt;h4&gt;
                        Kalamkaari&lt;/h4&gt;
                    &lt;h5&gt;
                        Origin&lt;/h5&gt;
                    &lt;p&gt;
                        ‘Kalam’ meaning pen and ‘Kari’ meaning work, Kalam-kari the 3000-year-old traditional
                        craft of Andhra Pradesh, originated from Srikalahasti and Machilipatnam.&lt;/p&gt;
                    &lt;h5&gt;
                        Distinct&lt;/h5&gt;
                    &lt;p&gt;
                        The Srikalahasti style, drawing inspiration from temples and Hindu mythologies,
                        involves graphics using the pen alone. The second (Machilipatnam) has a distinct
                        Islamic influence involving work with the pen as well as engraved wooded blocks.&lt;/p&gt;
                    &lt;p&gt;
                        While the former style has flourished with the villages developed into a craftsmen’s
                        hub, the Machilipatnam tradition lags behind and the future of this Islamic style
                        of the art looks dark.&lt;/p&gt;
