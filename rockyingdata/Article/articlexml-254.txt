  <Text>&lt;ol&gt;
    &lt;li&gt;
        &lt;h3&gt;
            Ants cant stand Salt&lt;/h3&gt;
        &lt;img src="http://www.rockying.com/art/Do-It-Yourself-Slide-Show/Good-Uses-of-Salt/1-deter-ants.jpg"
            alt="Deter Ants" /&gt;
        &lt;p&gt;
            Sprinkle salt around ants path or cracks.&lt;/p&gt;
    &lt;/li&gt;
    &lt;li&gt;
        &lt;h3&gt;
            Grass and Weeds hate Salt as well&lt;/h3&gt;
        &lt;img src="http://www.rockying.com/art/Do-It-Yourself-Slide-Show/Good-Uses-of-Salt/2-killing-grass-and-weeds-growing-in-cracks-in-your-driveway.jpg"
            alt="Salt kilss grass and weeds" /&gt;
        &lt;p&gt;
            Put salt on grass patch and pour hot water.&lt;/p&gt;
    &lt;/li&gt;
    &lt;li&gt;
        &lt;h3&gt;
            Get Rid of Fleas&lt;/h3&gt;
        &lt;img src="http://www.rockying.com/art/Do-It-Yourself-Slide-Show/Good-Uses-of-Salt/3-say-goodbye-to-fleas.jpg"
            alt="Bye bye fleas" /&gt;
        &lt;p&gt;
            Wash your pets blanket, house or resting area with salt water. Sprinkle some salt
            on rugs.
        &lt;/p&gt;
    &lt;/li&gt;
    &lt;li&gt;
        &lt;h3&gt;
            Picking dropped eggs&lt;/h3&gt;
        &lt;img src="http://www.rockying.com/art/Do-It-Yourself-Slide-Show/Good-Uses-of-Salt/4-pick-up-a-dropped-egg.jpg"
            alt="" /&gt;
        &lt;p&gt;
            If you drop an egg on the kitchen floor, sprinkle salt on the mess and leave it
            there for 20 minutes. You’ll be able to wipe it right up.&lt;/p&gt;
    &lt;/li&gt;
    &lt;li&gt;
        &lt;h3&gt;
            Clean up oven spills quickly&lt;/h3&gt;
        &lt;img src="http://www.rockying.com/art/Do-It-Yourself-Slide-Show/Good-Uses-of-Salt/5-clean-up-oven-spills-quickly.jpg"
            alt="" /&gt;&lt;p&gt;
                If a pie or casserole bubbles over in the oven, pour a handful of salt on top of
                the spill. It won’t smoke, smell and, most importantly, will bake into a crust that
                makes the mess easier to clean once it’s cooled.&lt;/p&gt;
    &lt;/li&gt;
    &lt;li&gt;
        &lt;h3&gt;
            Clean brown spots off your iron&lt;/h3&gt;
        &lt;img src="http://www.rockying.com/art/Do-It-Yourself-Slide-Show/Good-Uses-of-Salt/6clean-brown-spots-off-your-iron.jpg"
            alt="" /&gt;&lt;p&gt;
                Simply sprinkle salt on a sheet of waxed paper, slide the iron across it and rub
                lightly with silver polish. Your iron will look like brand-new in no time.&lt;/p&gt;
    &lt;/li&gt;
    &lt;li&gt;
        &lt;h3&gt;
            Remove stains from your coffee pot&lt;/h3&gt;
        &lt;img src="http://www.rockying.com/art/Do-It-Yourself-Slide-Show/Good-Uses-of-Salt/7-remove-stains-from-your-coffee-pot.jpg"
            alt="" /&gt;&lt;p&gt;
                Fill it with 1/4 cup of table salt and a dozen ice cubes. Swish the mixture around,
                let it sit for half an hour, fill it with cold water and rinse. Your coffee pot
                will look brand new.&lt;/p&gt;
    &lt;/li&gt;
    &lt;li&gt;
        &lt;h3&gt;
            Keep your windshield frost-free&lt;/h3&gt;
        &lt;img src="http://www.rockying.com/art/Do-It-Yourself-Slide-Show/Good-Uses-of-Salt/8-keep-your-windshield-frost-free.jpg"
            alt="" /&gt;
        &lt;p&gt;
            Dip a sponge into salt water and rub it on windows, and they won’t frost up even
            when the mercury drops below zero.&lt;/p&gt;
    &lt;/li&gt;
    &lt;li&gt;
        &lt;h3&gt;
            Shell nuts more easily&lt;/h3&gt;
        &lt;img src="http://www.rockying.com/art/Do-It-Yourself-Slide-Show/Good-Uses-of-Salt/9-shell-nuts-more-easily.jpg"
            alt="" /&gt;
        &lt;p&gt;
            Soak pecans and walnuts in salt water for a few hours before shelling them. Doing
            so will make it easier to remove the meat.&lt;/p&gt;
    &lt;/li&gt;
    &lt;li&gt;
        &lt;h3&gt;
            Drip-proof candles&lt;/h3&gt;
        &lt;img src="http://www.rockying.com/art/Do-It-Yourself-Slide-Show/Good-Uses-of-Salt/10-drip-proof-candles.jpg"
            alt="" /&gt;
        &lt;p&gt;
            If you soak new candles in a strong salt solution for a few hours, then dry them
            well, they won’t drip when you burn them.&lt;/p&gt;
    &lt;/li&gt;
&lt;/ol&gt;</Text>
